Our weekly Activities here at Mayapur Bace

dharshan

Darshan, which in Sanskrit means ‘viewing’, holds immense significance in Indian philosophy and religious beliefs. Darshan consists of beholding a deity, especially in image form. Devotees believe that by having darshan of the Deity's image, one receives the positive energy that is given by the deity, also known as blessings.

Kritan

Kirtan, a form of devotional call-and-response chanting popularized in India five hundred years ago by Sri Chaitanya Mahaprabhu, is regularly practiced by ISKCON devotees and has now spread throughout the world through the efforts of the Vaishnava community.

Prasadam

It is an expression of love, devotion and compassion in the form of food items. Devotees who aspire to pure consciousness, Individuals prepare food for the Lord and then offer it to the Lord with love and devotion.

Bhagwat Geeta

The Bhagavad Gita, an ancient sacred text of India, is universally regarded as a major source of spiritual wisdom and inspiration for mankind. Spoken directly by Lord Shri Krishna to his intimate friend Arjuna, the seven hundred verses of the Bhagavad Gita provide a definitive guide to the science of self-realization.
